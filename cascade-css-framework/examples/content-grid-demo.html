Default Content Width
All direct children of .content-grid automatically span the content area. This is the readable content zone, typically around 65ch wide.
.content--feature
Breaks out into the feature columns (default: 50px on each side). Great for pull quotes, small images, or emphasis.
.content--feature-max
Breaks out further into the feature-max columns (default: additional 100px on each side). Ideal for larger images or highlighted content.
.content--full
Full-bleed from edge to edge. Perfect for hero images, full-width backgrounds, or immersive media. Note: no padding - goes right to the viewport edge.
.content--full-safe
Full-width but with safe gutters. Spans the full width while maintaining comfortable padding from viewport edges. Best of both worlds.
Back to Content Width
Elements automatically return to content width. The grid flows naturally between different contexts.

The Power of Content-Grid

Content-grid gives you precise control over how content breaks out of the standard reading width. Unlike traditional containers, it uses CSS Grid's named lines to create semantic breakout zones.

"The content-grid pattern is one of the most elegant solutions for long-form content with breakout elements."

The seven columns provide five distinct content zones: full-bleed, feature-max, feature, content, and their mirrored counterparts on the right side. This creates natural rhythm and visual interest in your layouts.

<section class="content-grid">
    <h1>Default content width</h1>
    <p>Paragraphs stay readable.</p>

    <img class="content--feature-max" src="hero.jpg" alt="Breaks out wider">

    <div class="content--full">
        Full-bleed element
    </div>
</section>

The grid automatically handles responsive behavior. On smaller screens, the breakout columns collapse gracefully while maintaining the gutter spacing.
